What Vet INC does

Vet owners have one big problem: their prices go stale. They're busy with patients all day. Price updates get pushed back. Nobody notices until revenue is already leaking.

The pain vets feel:

  • Prices haven't been reviewed in months — sometimes over a year
  • The clinic down the street quietly raised their fees and nobody noticed
  • Changing a price means digging through AVImark/Cornerstone or a spreadsheet
  • They're underpriced and losing revenue they don't even know about
  • The "price review" keeps getting pushed because there's always a patient waiting
What we built: An app that lets vet owners see, manage, and update their prices quickly — so it stops being something they have to find time for. Jack built this for his own family's vet clinics first.
You are NOT selling the product. You're selling a 20-minute conversation with Jack. That's it. Don't explain features, don't quote prices, don't demo anything. Book the call.

Your daily workflow

Everything happens on the portal at team.vetinc.ca. Here's your day:

  • Log in with your email and password
  • See today's leads — automatically loaded for you (up to 50/day)
  • Pick a lead and click "Start call"
  • The call script appears — personalized for that clinic with doctor name, clinic name, and timezone
  • Before dialing: check the clinic website if no doctor/owner is listed. Look at About, Team, Doctors, Leadership pages
  • Call the clinic and follow the script
  • When done, click "Call ended" and log the outcome
  • Move to the next lead
Timezone chip: Each lead shows the clinic's local time. Green ✓ = good time to call (7–9 AM, 12–1 PM, 5–6 PM their time). Amber ⚠ = not ideal but still OK. Always confirm their timezone before booking.
3

Getting past the receptionist

The receptionist is not your enemy. They're doing their job. Your job is to get past them respectfully to the owner or doctor.

  • Opener: "Hey — quick question for Dr. [LastName], is Dr. [LastName] around?"
  • If "what's it about": "It's about the clinic's pricing — I need two minutes with [Owner]."
  • If "who are you": "[Your Name] from Vet INC. We built an app that lets vet owners update their prices without digging through their PMS."
  • If "is this a sales call": "No — it's just a short intro call. Jack handles everything from there."
  • If owner unavailable: "No problem — are they easier to reach mornings or afternoons?" Get their name, best callback time, and email.
  • If "send an email": "Happy to — what's the best email for [Owner]?" Then call back in 48 hours.
Never pitch the receptionist. They can't book the call. Be polite, be brief, get to the owner.

7

Booking & logging calls

When a clinic agrees to talk with Jack:

  • Open Calendar — click the blue button to open Jack's Calendly
  • Let the clinic owner pick a time that works for them
  • Click "Call ended" and select "Meeting booked"
  • Enter the scheduled date/time so it appears on Jack's calendar
  • Log any notes from the conversation
  • Click "Save outcome"
Every call must be logged. Even if they didn't answer. Even if they said no. Click "Call ended," pick the outcome, and save. This is how Jack tracks progress and how you get credit for your work.
