What Vet INC does

Vet owners have one big problem: their prices go stale. They're busy with patients all day. Price updates get pushed back. Nobody notices until revenue is already leaking.

The pain vets feel:

  • Prices haven't been reviewed in months — sometimes over a year
  • The clinic down the street quietly raised their fees and nobody noticed
  • Changing a price means digging through AVImark/Cornerstone or a spreadsheet
  • They're underpriced and losing revenue they don't even know about
What we built: An app that lets vet owners see, manage, and update their prices quickly — so it stops being something they have to find time for. Jack built this for his own family's vet clinics first.
You are NOT selling the product. You're selling a 20-minute conversation with Jack. That's it. Don't explain features, don't quote prices, don't demo anything. Book the call.

The new call script

The script is built into the portal — when you click "Start call" on a lead, it personalizes everything for that clinic. Here's what to know:

Before calling Check the clinic website first for names (owner/vet, office manager). Confirm timezone before booking anything. Call timing matters Best windows: 7:00–8:30 AM, 12:00–1:00 PM, 4:00–4:30 PM (their time). Avoid mid-morning (9:30–11:30ish) and last 30 min before close. Step 1: Find the pricing decision-maker Target the office/practice manager first — they evaluate tools before the owner. Opening (whoever picks up) "Hi, this is [YOUR NAME] calling from VetInc — quick question: when a fee needs updating at the clinic, does that usually go through you, or does [OWNER] handle it directly?" If they ARE the pricing-owner (Branch A) "Got it — is now an okay time for a two-minute rundown? Or 10 minutes another time?" Pitch: "This isn't about making pricing harder -- it's about making it easy. VetInc lets you see and update your entire fee schedule in minutes, without digging through your PMS or waiting for a slow day that never comes." Close: "Worth 20 minutes with Jack directly to see if it's a fit?" Get: name, callback time, direct email. If they are NOT the pricing-owner (Branch B) Don't pitch. Use them as intel. "Is pricing something that's come up much?" "Is [OWNER] the one who'd look at this, or someone else?" Get: name, callback time, direct email. Vet/Owner (when you reach them) "When was the last time you sat down and went through your full price list?" "Jack had the same problem at his family's clinics. Worth 20 minutes? [DAY] morning or afternoon?" Always get the owner's direct email — no email = not a real lead.

Your daily workflow

Everything happens on the portal at team.vetinc.ca. Here's your day:

  • Log in with your email and password
  • See today's leads — automatically loaded for you (up to 50/day)
  • Pick a lead and click "Start call"
  • The call script appears — personalized for that clinic
  • Before dialing: check the clinic website if no owner is listed
  • Call and follow the script
  • When done, click "Call ended" and log the outcome
  • Move to the next lead
Timezone chip: Each lead shows the clinic's local time. Always call in their timezone and confirm before booking.

The rules

  • Always get direct email — no email = not a real lead
  • One job: book Jack's 20-minute call. Do not sell the product.
  • Pricing-owner first, vet second. Never pitch someone who doesn't handle pricing.
  • Use Dr. LastName only when it's actually a doctor.
  • Confirm timezone before booking any call.
  • Always close with two specific time options (morning or afternoon).
